Visual Culture and Visual Communications in the Context of Globalization

Winkler Dietmar R, 2009

Visible Language 43, no. 1: 4-18, 20-22, 24-43

Link

In the article, Dietmar critically examines the limitations of current communication design education and practice, and argues for ways that can better support intercultural communication and protect cultural diversity. He points out the distinct differences of diverse cultures and societies and the difficulties of dissecting them. It states that globalisation makes these challenges worse by eroding the diversity of culture, especially when the stronger ones cover the voice of the weaker ones for specific reasons. Dietmar appeals that cultural communication requires creators’ dedication to understanding and respecting different backgrounds and being concerned with the viewers. Educating the public and teaching them how to engage in intercultural communication in their daily lives are also necessary.

Because Shigeo Fukuda’s design works have the ambition and potential to reach farther among people of complex cultural and social backgrounds, reading this article may help us discuss, from a critical approach, how to improve the intercultural visual design and communication, the challenges and risks of design developed in the context of globalisation, and the possible limitations of design in intercultural communication.
