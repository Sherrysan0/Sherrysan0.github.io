Swiss Graphic Design : The Origins and Growth of an International Style

Richard Hollis, 2006

London: Laurence King Publishing Ltd

This book focuses on the period from the Swiss graphic design style’s origin in the 1920s to the 1960s when it was exported to become an international style acknowledged by the world. Hollis Richard traces the growth of the style in the social and cultural context of this particular period. He tells how the style formed and depicts its characteristics with the reasons behind them. He also explores the development of significant Swiss designers and their works to contribute to this style.

Because Shigeo Fukuda has widely used sans-serif typeface and often referred to minimalism and modernism in his poster design, this book can explore the Swiss style as a possible influencing factor to his design. The book also points out the possibility of the development of a graphic design style that attempts to be internationally orientated. However, Shigeo’s poster design focuses on his illusionary illustrations while the Swiss style is dominated by the use of photography, so the book probably doesn’t help in this aspect.
