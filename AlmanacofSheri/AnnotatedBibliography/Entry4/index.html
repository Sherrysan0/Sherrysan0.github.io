Anything Goes with Wit and Ambiguity: Playfulness in Japanese Visual Culture

Hung Ky Nguyen, 2020

Design Issues 36, no. 2: 72–86

Link

'Playfulness' is one of the eight characteristics of Japanese Design Movement Posters. Ky Nguyen’s article analyses the significance and distinctiveness of playfulness in Japanese art and design from cultural, political, social, and philosophical perspectives. It also depicts how playfulness has emerged and been used. The author states that during the three key periods in Japanese history, playfulness was used widely in the works of Japanese artists and designers to establish cultural identity, influence, and individual characteristics. He suggests that Japanese Buddhism’s extensive use of playfulness in Buddhist teaching and The Heart Sutra’s emphasis on the non-difference of the opposites also make playfulness not only be used to express the positive under the Japanese sensibility. On the other hand, intending to express individuality and Japanese sensibilities, Japanese Design Movement poster designers often express playfulness through themes, compositions, techniques, and contexts.

Based on Japanese visual culture and history, this article may inspire the possible reasons why playfulness and humour are widely used in Shigeo Fukuda’s design works and even in works depicting political and war themes.
