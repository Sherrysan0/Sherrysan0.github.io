The Bauhaus: 1919-1933

Magdalena Droste, 2019

Cologne: Taschen

In the book, Magdalena intends to accurately document the 14-year history of the Bauhaus as a School of Art and Design from 1919, when the Bauhaus founded in Weimar, Germany, to the end of the Bauhaus in 1933. She focuses on the respective goals, characteristics, and principles in the school of three locations, which are Weimar, Dessau, and Berlin, and the key artists and designers with their teaching theories and works. She also introduced the social, cultural and historical background of the school. Various workshops and practices happened in the school are described to emphasise the leading position of the school to combine different fields and explore different kinds of works across media and approaches creatively.

From the 1930s, the Bauhaus, with its teaching theories, has greatly affected Japanese design and pushed the development of Japanese modernism. Because Shigeo Fukuda often referred to minimalism and modernism in his design and also had explorations of different media, this book can help to explore the Bauhaus as a possible factor influencing his design.
