Research Statement



The research focuses on one key Japanese designer, Shigeo Fukuda, who is a sculptor, graphic artist, and poster designer interested in optical illusions. He was active mainly between 1956 and 2009. His activities as a poster designer are highlighted in the research.

This bibliography starts with Shigeo’s points of view to design both technically and conceptually. Then, it relates to the design elements and techniques in his works from various perspectives of Gestalt theory, the psychology of visual perception, social semiotics, and the history and development of optical illusion art. It also explores the historical and cultural backrgound behind him and factors that may affect him, which include the history and theories of Japanese poster design, Swiss graphic design, and the Bauhaus in Germany. Finally, it discusses visual design in an international context and the difficulties and challenges of design works like Shigeo’s, which are intended to reach the public of complex cultural and social backgrounds.
