Japanese Posters: The First 100 Years

Richard S Thornton, 1989

Design Issues 6, no. 1: 4–14

Link

In this article, Richard describes the history of poster design in Japan over the past 100 years, from the first poster 'bira' that appeared, combining traditional Japanese calligraphy and images during the 19th century, to the 1970s, when Japanese poster design was playing an important role in the international design world. The author points out the influence of Western on Japanese poster design in technique, aesthetics, and concept, thus pushing it to the global stage. Richard states that modernism, especially the Bauhaus, has also affected Japanese design and its teaching theories a lot to develop an international style.

This article helps us to briefly understand the historical, cultural, and social factors that may have influenced Shigeo Fukuda’s poster design and his international style from the aspect of the history of Japanese poster design and its development.
