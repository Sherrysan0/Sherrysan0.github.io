Art and Visual Perception

Rudolf Arnheim, 2004

California: University of California Press

In this book, Rudolf tries to prove that Gestalt theory and the psychology of visual perception can be used to analyse the visual process and interpret art activities. Visual perception is the integrated response of the brain to what the eye sees in Gestalt theory. The book provides a comprehensive description of how the various visual elements in a picture, such as balance, shape, space, and light, affect the viewer’s psyche and emotions. Rudolf suggests that the subjective feelings that the audience gets from viewing an artwork are due to the expressive natures in the structure of the work itself. The creative reproduction of the artist based on the perception and grasp of reality is also emphasised.

This book helps us understand, from the perspective of Gestalt theory and the psychology of visual perception, the basic visual elements used in Shigeo Fukuda’s design works, such as balance, shape, figure-ground, and simplification, and how he uses them to make his works easy to understand, and also influence the subjective feelings of viewers.
