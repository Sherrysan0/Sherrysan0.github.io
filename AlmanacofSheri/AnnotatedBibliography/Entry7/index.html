Reading Images: The Grammar of
Visual Design 3rd ed

Gunther Kress & Theo van Leeuwen, 2020

London: Routledge

In the book, 'social semiotics' is used as a theoretical structure to discuss how to communicate with images. The authors seek to develop a framework that can be used to analyse and present the regularities of visual communication. The “grammar of the visual” focuses on analysing how to arrange visual elements to produce meaning in contemporary images. The book points out that 'reading images' and interpreting images are similar to 'making signs', which is the process to constitute a sign/ metaphor.

This book allows us to understand how signs are used to make visual compositions and communicate meaning from a social semiotics perspective. Thus, we may understand better the various signs and metaphors used in the Shigeo Fukuda’s poster design to imply and visualise his design concepts. However, the context of this book is based on Western cultures and the history of Western visual semiotics. There are limitations to the analysis of Fukuda’s design decisions, which are influenced by his Asian background.
