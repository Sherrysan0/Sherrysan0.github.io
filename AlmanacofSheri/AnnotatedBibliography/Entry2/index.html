Masters of Deception : Escher, Dalí & The Artists of Optical Illusion

Al Seckel, 2004

New York: Sterling Pub. Co

In this book, Al intends to introduce optical illusion art with its development in both techniques and concepts through history with artists who pursue it. He collects some artists working in the particular areas of optical illusion art from the early modern period to the present. Shigeo Fukuda is also included in this collection as the author introduces his life experience in design and presents his works related to optical illusions. He points out that Shigeo used anamorphoses, impossible figures and objects, figure/ground reversals, composite images, shadow sculptures, and accidental alignments.

This book can help us briefly know about the history of illusion art and the artists’ exploration of creatively using optical illusion in their works. Thus, we may understand the possible factors that influenced Shigeo to exploit optical illusions in his works, such as the artist Giuseppe Arcimboldo, that Shigeo created sculptures about his work in a creative approache.
