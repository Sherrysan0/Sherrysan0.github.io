This image displays the Bauhaus Dessau building designed by Walter Adolph Georg Gropius, who was the founder of the Bauhaus School.
The design of this building was intended to present the spirit and image of the Bauhaus, showing an integration of aesthetics and function.1 Several wings of different sizes are connected in the building. The building is divided into clear areas with distinct functions, with materials and design responding to their nature.2 The glazed facade outside the four-storey workshop creates a strong reflection, thus bringing a transparent and gleaming visual effect and a spectacular aesthetic feeling.
