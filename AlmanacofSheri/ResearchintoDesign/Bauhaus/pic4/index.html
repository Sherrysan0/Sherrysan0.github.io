Herbert Bayer, Design for a universal type, 1926.



Herbert Bayer attended the Bauhaus in Weimar from 1921 to 1923. The Universal alphabet designed by him in this image expresses an ambition to develop an international style and combine the form and function of a typeface.1 This sansserif typeface only has lowercase letters. Its simple and consistent design relied on the wide use and emphasis of the basic circular shape. The strokes are thick and consistent, and the x-height is relatively low to increase readability. It can also help to make the typography and typesetting easier.
