Bauhaus



‘Bauhaus’ was the name of a school of art and design founded in 1919 in Germany, by a German architect Walter Gropius and was demanded to be closed by the Nazis in 1933. The school’s various workshops and practices showed a combination of different fields and experiments across media and approaches creatively.1 It made significant contributions to many areas of art and design. Bauhaus also became a name for a style based on the school’s distinct aesthetic characteristics and principles.
