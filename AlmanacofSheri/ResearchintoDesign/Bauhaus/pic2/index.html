Gustav Hassenpflug, Folding table in three different positions, 1928.



Gustav Hassenpflug studied at the Bauhaus from 1926 to 1929. The interior design in the Bauhaus focused on ‘greatest possible cost-effectiveness’ and practicality in people’s daily life.1 The design should be ‘standard’2 and can be used in mass production. This table is made of relatively cheap plywood. Its centre single leg is a combination of 4 pieces of L-like legs. Both the tabletop and legs can be folded to a 9 cm-wide flat ‘board’ for easier storage. The design is simple and geometric, flexible and convenient for use, presenting a harmony of form and function.

  1. Magdalena Droste, The Bauhaus: 1919-1933 (Taschen, Berlin, 2019), 367-368.
