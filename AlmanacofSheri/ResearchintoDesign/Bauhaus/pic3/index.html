Oskar Schlemmer, Group photo of Triadic Ballet as part of the revue Wieder Metropol in the Metropol Theatre, 1926.



Triadic Ballet is a ballet developed by Oskar Schlemmer, who worked as Master of Form at the Bauhaus theatre workshop from 1923. This work presents an integration of abstract forms and performance design based on the Bauhaus trend.1 The costume design in this photograph shows a wide use of geometric shapes and repetition. The texture is smooth and glossy, looking like metal and machines. The costume ‘standardised’ and restricted both the individual expression and the movement of the dancers.2 Thus, the dancers became the ‘medium’3 for this abstract and symbolic performance design.
