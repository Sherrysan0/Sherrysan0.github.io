Inscribed Human Skull Chia 3739, late Shang period.



This image shows an inscribed human skull with a character from the oracle bone script on it. It was discovered in Henan, China, and speculated to be from the late Shang period. The character means ‘武’, which relates to ‘fighting’ in Chinese. Its upper part is the pictograph of ‘戈’, representing the weapon, and the lower part is the pictograph of ‘止’, representing the toe, so the combination means someone holding the weapon and walking.1 This is an example of pictographs used as part of a written character to represent meanings without relating to the speech.2 However, its understanding relies on the particular context.
