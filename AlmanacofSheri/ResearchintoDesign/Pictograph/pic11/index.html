“Large Engraved Horse,” The Chauvet-Pont d’Arc cave, 30,000– 32,000 BP.



This image displays a finger-drawing of a horse on the soft clay film, now carbonated. It was discovered in the Chauvet- Pont d’Arc cave in Ardèche, France, and was speculated to be from the Aurignacian period.1 This animal image used only lines to abstract the horse’s structure, but it can still be identified as a horse with its elongated head, long thin legs and muscular body. This shows an early pictograph created by human beings to depict the environment around them.
