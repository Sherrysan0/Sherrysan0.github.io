Venus von Willendorf, c.30,000 BP.



This image presents a sculpture, named Venus von Willendorf. It was discovered in Lower Austria, and was speculated to be from around 29,500 years ago. It shows an abstract and exaggerated depiction of a female human figure. The female characteristics of large and saggy breasts and belly are highlighted. It shows an early visual expression of a human figure through the three-dimensional form that can be related to the development of pictograph.
