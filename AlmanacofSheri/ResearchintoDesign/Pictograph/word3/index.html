Pictograph



‘Pictograph’ is a term used to describe an image that communicates information through its visual form.1 The visual form can be a picture, drawing, photograph, symbol, pictogram or ideogram. The information expressed can be a word, a phrase, or more complex and equivocal.
A successful communication among human beings using pictographs may include a series of symbols that are easy to identify or conventional in particular communities.2 Pictograph should be distinguished from writing, which is used to record linguistic statements.3

  1. Johanna Drucker and Jerome McGann, “Images as the Text: Pictographs and Pictographic Logic,” Information Design Journal 10, no. 2 (June 2001): 95, https://www.researchgate.net/publication/233661565_Images_as_the_text_Pictographs_and_pictographic_rhetoric.
