Hans Holbein the Younger, The Ambassadors, 1533.




Photographer: Steven Zucker.



‘Anamorphosis’1 is used in Hans Holbein the Younger’s The Ambassadors. There is an elongated shape at the bottom of the painting, but when the audience views it at a particular angle relative to the right side of the picture plane,2 it transforms to be a skull. Consequently, when viewing the painting in the front, the shape should be part of the ground surrounding the figure, but when viewing the side, it becomes the figure, and the oblique painting is the ground. The painting shows how anamorphosis can affect the figure/ground relationship.
