“The Gods Osiris and Atum,” Tomb of Nefertari, ca.1250 BCE.



In this image, all the objects in this part of the Egyptian frescoes are depicted to be abstract and flat. Thus, the painting can be divided into only two planes of figure and ground. The figure panel is all the objects with lines as ‘visual boundaries’,1 and the ground panel is the blank part outside the depicted objects. The figure/ground relationship can be very clear in this flat drawing using lines.
