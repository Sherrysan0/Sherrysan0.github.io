Shigeo Fukuda, Illusion poster for exhibition, 1975.



In this image of Shigeo Fukuda’s poster, he created a ‘figure/ ground perceptual reversal’.1 The poster is equally divided into the top white and bottom black parts. The legs are interspersed in the boundaries. When the audience is focusing on the black legs, the white parts become the ground, while when they are focusing on the white legs, the black parts become the ground. It reveals how the principles of the figure/ground relationship can be used to create visual illusions.2
