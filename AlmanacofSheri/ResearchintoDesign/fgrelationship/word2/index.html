Figure/Ground Relationship



From the perspective of Gestalt theory and the psychology of visual perception, ‘figure/ground relationship’ is a term used to describe the relationship between two kinds of planes on a picture, one called ‘figure’ and the other called ‘ground’.1 Usually, ‘figure’ is the main subject, such as the human figure, while ‘ground’ is the background used to depict or emphasise the main subject. The figure/ground relationship can be used to depict the distance or space relationship between the figure and the ground from the view of the observer.2
